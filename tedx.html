TedxYouth @ DPS RK Puram 2016

March 2016

TED is a media organisation which posts talks online for free distribution under the slogan “ideas worth spreading”. Every year the TEDxYouth club at Delhi Public School, R.K Puram hosts an independently organised TEDx event. As the team's graphic designer, I was responsible for putting together the identity cards, banners, speaker profiles and posters.

Figure 1 : The Onstage Banner

Just like any other event, this event also needed to be given its very own identity. Now, the question is - how do you give an identity to a non-living thing. The answer - consistency in design. TED already uses red as their primary color, therefore, I decided to go with using red and white. The visuals were a little tricky, but I decided to use simple and minimal elements to create patterns and shapes that could be used throughout the visual design for the event.

Figure 2 : The cover for the annual brochure

Once the designs were finalised, we had to arrange for the printing. This involved calling up multiple printers, getting the costing, bargaining, making sure we were spending withing our allotted budget. After all the hardwork (with a hint of struggling), everything got printed and this is what it looked like.
