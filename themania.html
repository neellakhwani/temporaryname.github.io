The Mania

December 2017

The brief given by my college was to either redesign an existing game or to create your own game from scratch. I decided on redesigning UNO, the game by Mattel. UNO was created by Merle Robbins. The story goes that he got into an argument with his son about a similar card game called Crazy Eights. Crazy Eights is a shredding type card game for two to seven players. The objective of the game is to be the first one to get rid of all the cards to a discard pile. Uno is a fun game to play with friends and family and has always been one of my favorite card games.

Figure 1 : Rough designs for the cards

Once the design was finalised on paper, I proceeded with making the designs digitally and playing around with the color schemes. This bit was slightly challenging since Uno is already pretty well designed. I had to make decisions with extreme caution and make sure that I didn't subconsciously end up making it look like the original game itself.

Figure 2 : Digitalising the ideas

After the entire process of digitalisation was done, I had to arrange for the printing. This involved calling up multiple printers, getting the costing, bargaining, making sure that I could afford to get the cards printed without spending all of my pocket money. After all the hardwork (with a hint of struggling), everything got printed and this is what it looked like.
